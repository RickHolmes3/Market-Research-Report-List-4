<p><p>Optical spectrometers are instruments used to analyze the interaction of light with matter. There are two main types of optical spectrometers: atomic spectrometry and molecular spectrometry. </p><p>Atomic spectrometry involves analyzing the electromagnetic radiation absorbed or emitted by atoms, providing information on the elemental composition of a sample. Molecular spectrometry, on the other hand, focuses on the interaction of light with molecules, providing insights into the molecular structure and composition of a substance. Both types of spectrometers play crucial roles in various fields such as chemistry, biology, and environmental science.</p></p>
