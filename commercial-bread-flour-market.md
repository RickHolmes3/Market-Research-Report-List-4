<p><p>Commercial bread flour market offers two main types of flour: Machine-Milled Flour and Stone-Ground Flour. Machine-milled flour is made using industrial machines that grind the grains into fine particles, resulting in a consistent texture and higher gluten content, ideal for making fluffy bread. On the other hand, stone-ground flour is produced by grinding grains between two stone wheels, which retains more nutrients and flavor but may vary in texture and gluten content. Both types cater to different preferences and baking needs in the commercial bread industry.</p></p>
