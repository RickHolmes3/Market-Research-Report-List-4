Convex Lenses are thicker at the center and thinner at the edges, converging light rays to a focal point. Concave Lenses are thinner at the center and thicker at the edges, diverging light rays. Both types of lenses have specific applications depending on the desired light manipulation and focusing requirements in the market.</p></p>
