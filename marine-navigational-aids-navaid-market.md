<p><p>Marine Navigational Aids (NAVAIDs) encompass various types of equipment and structures that help guide ships safely through waterways. Buoys are floating markers that indicate navigation hazards or designate channels. Lighthouses are tall structures with powerful lights to signify coastlines or dangerous areas. Fog signals emit sounds to aid navigation in low visibility conditions. Day beacons are fixed markers on land or in the water. Other types of NAVAIDs include radio beacons, GPS systems, and radar systems that provide additional guidance for marine vessels.</p></p>
