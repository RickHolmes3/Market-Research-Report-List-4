<p><p>High load ball screws are available in two main types: rolled and ground. Rolled ball screws are manufactured using a rolling process, making them more cost-effective than ground ball screws. They are suitable for applications with moderate precision requirements. On the other hand, ground ball screws are manufactured using a grinding process, resulting in higher precision and better performance in high-load applications. They are more expensive than rolled ball screws but offer superior accuracy and durability. Both types cater to different needs in the high load ball screw market.</p></p>
