<p><p>Spatial Light Modulators (SLMs) are devices that modify the amplitude, phase, or polarization of light waves. Reflective SLMs use mirrors to redirect light, while transmissive SLMs use liquid crystals or other materials to modulate light passing through. Reflective SLMs are often used in holography, projection, and adaptive optics applications, while transmissive SLMs are commonly found in devices like projectors and displays. Both types of SLMs play crucial roles in various industries, including telecommunications, automotive, and healthcare.</p></p>
