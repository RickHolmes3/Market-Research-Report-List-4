<p><p>Decentralized HVAC systems consist of multiple smaller units located throughout a building, offering greater flexibility and energy efficiency. Centralized HVAC systems, on the other hand, feature a single large unit that serves an entire building, providing consistent temperature control but potentially higher energy costs. Both systems are used in large-scale HVAC applications, with decentralized systems being favored for their zoning capabilities and centralized systems often chosen for their simplicity and ease of maintenance.</p></p>
