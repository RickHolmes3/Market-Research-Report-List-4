<p><p>Jet engines are classified into three main types based on their design and function. Turbojet engines are the simplest form, producing thrust by expelling hot exhaust gases. Turbofan engines are more efficient and quieter, with a large fan at the front that creates additional thrust. Turboprop engines use a propeller to generate thrust, making them ideal for slower aircraft. Each type of engine has its own specific market, with turbofan engines being popular in commercial airliners, turbojet engines in military aircraft, and turboprop engines in regional planes and small aircraft.</p></p>
