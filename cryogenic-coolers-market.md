<p><p>Cryogenic coolers are devices used to achieve extremely low temperatures for various applications. The main types of cryogenic coolers are Gifford-Mcmahon, Pulse-Tube, Stirling, Joule-Thomson, and Brayton cryocoolers. Gifford-Mcmahon cryocoolers use a piston compressor to achieve cooling, while Pulse-Tube cryocoolers utilize a regenerator to create low temperatures. Stirling cryocoolers operate using a Stirling cycle, Joule-Thomson coolers expand gas to achieve cooling, and Brayton cryocoolers use a gas cycle to achieve cryogenic temperatures. Other types of cryogenic coolers include 4K closed-cycle cryocoolers and dilution refrigerators.</p></p>
