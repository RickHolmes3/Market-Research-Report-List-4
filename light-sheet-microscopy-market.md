<p><p>Light sheet microscopy refers to a technique used for imaging biological samples with minimal phototoxicity. Various types of light sheet microscopy are popular in the market, including Selective Plane Illumination Microscopy (SPIM) and Dual-view Inverted Selective Plane Illumination Microscopy (DiSPIM). SPIM involves illuminating a single plane of the sample, while DiSPIM uses two opposing objectives for improved resolution. Other types of light sheet microscopy include variations and enhancements on these techniques, each offering unique advantages for imaging biological samples.</p></p>
